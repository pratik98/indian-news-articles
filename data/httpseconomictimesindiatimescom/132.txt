China has launched a competition to select the 10 ugliest buildings in the country. So far, 87 questionable buildings have been nominated, including a violin-shaped church and a hotel in Inner Mongolia that resembles a monstrous doll. For the past 11 years, the Chinese architecture website archcy.com has invited people to vote in the annual competition. In China, local and foreign architects have long been experimenting with mixed results.



The 12th edition of its annual ‘Ugliest Building Survey’ the most voted must be analyzed by a commission of renowned architects in the country, which will choose the ten winners to be announced at the end of the year. According to the organizers, the proposal of the dispute is to provoke critical thinking about beauty and ugliness in architecture and promote the social responsibility of architects.
